About KUSU

Who we are and what we stand for

KUSU is a registered trade union representing non-teaching staff in universities across Kenya, championing social justice, decent work and fair treatment for all members.

Our Mission

To organize and empower university staff in Kenya, negotiate collectively for improved terms and conditions of service, and safeguard members‚Äô economic, social and professional interests through social dialogue and solidarity.

Core Values

  • Unity and solidarity
  • Integrity and accountability
  • Service to members
  • Equity and non-discrimination

Key Functions

  • Collective bargaining & CBAs
  • Representation in disputes & grievances
  • Legal and advisory support
  • Capacity building & member education

Who we represent

KUSU covers non-teaching staff in Kenyan universities, including:

  • ICT staff and technicians
  • Finance, HR and administrative staff
  • Library, laboratory and technical staff
  • Drivers, security, grounds and support staff

Lecturers and academic staff are mainly represented under other unions such as UASU.

Membership

How to join or update your details

Membership is voluntary and open to all non-teaching university staff who meet the requirements. No member should be punished for joining a union.

Steps to join KUSU

1
Fill the official KUSU membership form (download from resources or get from branch officials).
2
Indicate your correct staff number, department, university and contact details.
3
Sign and submit the form to your KUSU branch office / officials for processing and payroll deduction.
4
Join official KUSU communication channels (WhatsApp, email lists, meetings) for updates.

Already a member but changed department, university or contact? Kindly update your details through the membership update form.
